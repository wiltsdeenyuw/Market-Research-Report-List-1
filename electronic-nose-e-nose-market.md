<p><p>The electronic nose (E-nose) market comprises several technologies, each utilizing different sensing principles. Metal-Oxide Semiconductor (MOS) sensors detect gas concentrations through changes in conductivity. Conducting Polymer (CP) sensors rely on resistance changes upon exposure to specific gases. Quartz Crystal Microbalance (QCM) sensors measure mass changes at a crystal frequency level, enabling precise detection of volatile compounds. Other emerging technologies include surface acoustic wave sensors and optical methods, diversifying the E-nose applications across industries like food safety, environmental monitoring, and healthcare.</p></p>
